How I translate patterns

Every behavior is a signal. Every signal has a meaning. Every meaning lives in the nervous system. Every pattern has a repair move. This is how I work.

The Pattern Translation Framework

Four layers. One system. This is how I help you see what's actually happening underneath the story.

Step 1

Signal

What behavior is showing up? What are they doing? What are you doing? This is the surface layer, the thing you can actually see and describe.

Most people get stuck here, arguing about the behavior instead of understanding what it means.

Example: "He withdraws every time I bring up how I feel."
üîç
Step 2

Meaning

What is the behavior actually trying to communicate? Every action is an attempt to meet a need, protect from a threat, or regulate an overwhelming feeling.

The meaning isn't always what it looks like. Withdrawal might mean "I'm flooded and can't process," not "I don't care."

Example: His withdrawal = nervous system flooding + shame about not having the "right" response + fear of making things worse.
‚ö°
Step 3

Nervous System

Where is the body in this moment? Fight, flight, freeze, fawn, or faint? The nervous system state determines what's possible. You can't think clearly when you're dysregulated.

This is why logic doesn't work in heated moments. The body has to come down first.

Example: He goes into dorsal vagal shutdown (freeze). She escalates into sympathetic activation (fight). They're in two different nervous system states, trying to have one conversation.

Step 4

Repair Move

What's the one thing that could shift this pattern? Not fixing everything, just the next right move. A script to use. A pause to take. A signal to send.

Repair moves are small, specific, and nervous-system-informed. They break the loop.

Example: Her repair move: "I can see you're overwhelmed. I'm going to pause and give you 20 minutes. I still want to talk. I'm not going anywhere."

His repair move: "I'm shutting down. I need a few minutes. I'll come back."
